Thought over happiness

Thu 03 April 2014
By zouzoujing

In Life.

Pleasure is short lived and feelings can be deceiving and not reliable. This is piece of information I have just obtained from the online course about Buddism. Buddism is a very old religion and it existed way before Christ time. It is thousands of years of wisdom but it is not really understood by many people, including myself. Therefore I am following the course and hope for more understanding.

Why do we feel fear, unhappiness or anxiety sometimes? It originates from our attachment to our existence, our social status of such. For example, before a presentation, we feel anxiety because we are afraid of not doing well and lose our social status. Is there a a cure? What would the life be like if we can live beyond our attachment with no desire, no attachment to outside world?

Do you sometimes expect that future would be better? Therefore you change jobs, homes, partners etc. But after you got what you wanted, your excitement decays and you feel the same as before again. A experiment has been done to simulate this effect. Level of Dopamine, which is a neurotransmitter, is measured in the monkeys when they are given orange juice. The moment when the monkeys have the orange juice is positively correlated with the dopamine level. In comparison, the similar test is done on a group of trained monkeys. This group of monkeys are trained to associate turning on of the light with obtaining orange juice afterwards. As a result, an increase in dopamine level is observed when the light is turned on, while no level change at the moment of being given the orange juice. If after the light is turned on, no orange juice is given to the trained monkeys, a dip in dopamine level is shown. I think this test explains why we sometimes do not feel happy when we good things happen to us, since we take it too much for granted.
