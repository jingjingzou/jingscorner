Reading list

Wed 13 November 2013
By zouzoujing

In Misc.

During my holiday in Algarve, I have finished 3 books: Country driving: a Chinese road trip by Peter Hessler; The kite runner by Khaled Hosseini; The little coffee shop in Kabul by Debbie Rodriguez; I enjoyed all of them very much. The book over China is more of a diary, recording what Peter Hessler has experienced during his stay in China. I chose this book intentional just to see China from a foreigner's point of view. Though there are not a lot of surprises for me in the book, I am really amazed by the close contact he had with the local people. He was really involved in their lives and befriended them and shared partially their life. The other two books are fictions set in Kabul with the background of Russian invasion and Taliban control. Great story line and very easy read. I had to cry several times while reading the kite runner since I was really touched by the loyalty and friendship of Hassan to Amir. After finishing the book, I watched the movie again. The movie is a lot less touching since it simply missed too many details. But guess that is always the case.

I will make a reading list of books of different countries. It can be real stories or fictions as long as it does truly reflect the real history and culture of its time.
